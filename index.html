About me

Projects

  • Sequential Behavior Prediction. We seek to investigate sequential information and dynamic properties to enhance the performance of behavior prediction and recommendation. We have improved Recurrent Neural Networks (RNN) and achieved state-of-the-art performances. Several papers are published in top conferences such as AAAI and SIGIR. Based on our model, a system called SAPE has been developed for predicting public security events. Our research achievements have attracted attention from companies such as iHealth and Jingdong, and are going to be applied in real applications.
  • Contextual Information Modeling. We seek to utilize contextual information for better behavior modeling and recommendation. Our research is based on Matrix Factorization (MF) and Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN). We proposed a method to incorporate CNN for user behavior modeling for the first time. Several papers are published in top conferences and journals such as AAAI, CIKM and TKDE. Our research achievements have attracted attention from companies such as Chunyuyisheng, and are going to be applied in real applications.
  • NICE: Network Information Credibility Evaluation. We seek to automatically evaluate credibility of information on social media and detect rumors. A system called NICE has been developed, and papers are published in top conferences such as AAAI.
  • DIG: Data Intelligence Gathering. DIG is a distributed computing and demonstration platform based on 973 program.
  • ComWit. We create an online platform called ComWit (http://www.comwit.cn/). It aims to bridge the gap between academic research and industrial demands. Projects of companies and research achievements can be matched automatically based on algorithms, and suitable researchers can be found for proper projects. Several projects are seeking or have already found suitable researchers on ComWit.
  • Computational Healthcare. We seek to promote personal healthcare with machine learning techonology. Collaborating with iHealth, we predict blood presure of patients with augmented Recurrent Neural Networks (RNN). Collaborating with Chunyuyisheng, we recommend suitble doctors to patients according to interrogation records with augmented Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN) model.
